About the Initiative

Documenting an Endangered Legacy

The Amul Heritage Digital Initiative (AHDI) is an international research collaboration dedicated to documenting and digitally preserving UNESCO World Heritage sites along the Zarafshan-Karakum Corridor — the Turkmenistan section of the ancient Silk Road (Merv–Amul route).

We employ cutting-edge photogrammetry, AI-powered 3D reconstruction, and immersive VR technologies to create high-fidelity digital twins of endangered archaeological monuments. These digital records serve as both scientific archives and educational resources for future generations.

Our work bridges the disciplines of archaeology, computer science, and cultural heritage preservation, combining Chinese expertise in AI and machine learning with on-the-ground fieldwork expertise in Turkmenistan.

Our Approach

Methodology

We combine three complementary digital technologies to produce comprehensive, reproducible, and publicly accessible records of Silk Road heritage sites.

📐
Photogrammetry

High-precision 3D capture using both professional-grade equipment and accessible mobile applications such as Polycam. Thousands of overlapping photographs are processed through structure-from-motion algorithms to produce dense point clouds and textured meshes at millimetre accuracy. Field documentation is conducted in situ with minimal site disturbance.

Structure-from-Motion Drone Survey Polycam LiDAR
🧠
AI Reconstruction

Machine learning algorithms optimise and enhance raw photogrammetric outputs — automating texture processing, resolving occluded areas, and improving geometric fidelity. All AI-assisted outputs are clearly distinguished from primary photogrammetric records. Models are exported in open GLTF/GLB formats for maximum interoperability.

Neural Radiance Fields Mesh Optimisation Texture Synthesis GLTF/GLB
🥽
Immersive Experience

Completed 3D models are deployed in VR/AR environments, enabling virtual site visits for researchers, educators, and the public worldwide. Interactive walkthroughs preserve the spatial experience of heritage sites for communities separated from them by distance, conflict, or restricted access.

Documented Sites

Research Projects

Each project produces a multi-layered digital archive: raw photogrammetric datasets, optimised 3D models, and immersive VR applications — all preserved in open formats.

🏛
✓ Completed 2020
University Museum · Turkmenabat

Archaeology & Geology Museum

Digital documentation of the museum collections at Magtymguly Turkmen State University — establishing the core photogrammetric workflow that has underpinned all subsequent AHDI projects.

  • Complete artifact catalogue digitisation
  • Museum-grade 3D models for archival research
  • Workflow development and validation
  • Digital preservation for future accessibility
🏰
✓ Completed 2021
Parthian Era · 3rd Century BCE · Ahal Province

Ancient Nisa Fortress

Comprehensive 3D documentation of the Parthian capital — one of the most significant archaeological sites in Central Asia. The project addressed the complex multi-period stratigraphy of this UNESCO World Heritage site.

  • Complex architectural multi-layer documentation
  • Drone-assisted aerial photogrammetry
  • Digital preservation for archaeological research
  • Cross-referenced with Soviet-era excavation records
✦
✓ Completed 2024
Timurid Era · 15th Century · Turkmenabat

Seyitjemaleddin Mausoleum

A 15th-century architectural masterpiece of the Timurid period, renowned for its intricate glazed tilework. AHDI produced a high-resolution 3D model capturing original tile textures at sub-millimetre precision.

  • Sub-millimetre tile texture resolution
  • Full VR walkthrough capability
  • AI-enhanced reconstruction of damaged sections
  • Colour-accurate digital twin for conservation
